# Brand voice

How Manny Roy Consulting sounds in copy, docs, and UI. Use this when writing for the main site (Ghost), the docs site (Docusaurus), and any client-facing or internal content that represents the brand.

**Related:** [Brand foundation & positioning](brand-brainstorming.md), [Colours](colurs.md), [Fonts](fonts.md).

---

## 1. Personality

The brand blends **Architect**, **Pragmatist**, and **Thoughtful Builder**:

- **Calm** — No hype or urgency. Steady, clear, confident.
- **Direct** — Say what something is and why it matters. Avoid filler.
- **Analytical** — Ground claims in systems, data, and outcomes.
- **Warm** — Human and approachable. Not corporate; not cold.
- **Collaborative** — “We,” “together,” “with teams,” “partner.” Not lone genius.
- **Slightly playful** — Occasional wit or understatement. Never gimmicky or forced.

**Not:** Corporate, buzzword-heavy, hyper-agency, overly casual, or trendy-tech aesthetic.

---

## 2. Tone principles

### Confident but restrained

- State what you do and why it matters. No hedging (“we might be able to…”).
- Don’t oversell. No superlatives or “best-in-class” language.
- Let the work and structure speak. Short sentences and clear hierarchy.

### Clear and structured

- One idea per sentence or short paragraph.
- Use headings and lists so people can scan. Docs and pages should have a visible structure.
- Prefer concrete terms over abstract ones (“data pipelines” over “solutions”).

### Warm, but not chatty

- First person (“I,” “we”) is fine where it fits (About, Contact, CTAs).
- Friendly without being informal. No “hey!” or excessive exclamation.
- Reader-focused: “you” when addressing the reader; “I” when speaking for the brand.

### Professional with subtle personality

- Professional = reliable, precise, respectful of the reader’s time.
- Personality shows in word choice and rhythm, not in jokes or slang.
- Taglines and CTAs can be a bit more characterful (“Build once. Maintain well.”); body copy stays steady.

## 3. Messaging themes (use these, avoid the opposite)

| Use | Avoid |
|-----|--------|
| Tangled problems → clear systems | “Disruption,” “innovation,” “transform” |
| Sustainable, maintainable | Trend-chasing, quick fixes |
| Measurable impact, data-informed | Assumptions, folklore, hype |
| Collaboration, handoffs, clarity | Lone hero, black box |
| Prototype ≠ production; practical AI | “AI-powered everything,” demo theatre |
| Clean data, legible systems | Jargon for its own sake |

Recurring ideas across the site: **untangle**, **sustainable**, **grounded in data**, **measurable**, **clear**, **systems thinking**, **hold up over time**.

## 4. Voice by context

### Main site (Ghost) — Home, About, Services, Contact

- **Hero and CTAs:** Short, direct, benefit-led. One clear idea per line.
  - Good: “Technical systems built to last.” “Let’s build something sustainable.”
  - Avoid: Long sentences, multiple value props in one line.
- **Body and sections:** First person (“I,” “we”) where it’s about the consultant or the work. Warm but economical.
  - Good: “Technical systems are rarely just technical. They’re operational, financial, human, and often quietly political.”
  - Avoid: Third-person corporate (“Manny Roy Consulting is a leader in…”).
- **Lists and bullets:** Parallel structure. Concrete items, not vague “solutions.”

### Docs (Docusaurus) — Theme and application docs

- **Neutral and instructional.** “You” for the reader; “the theme,” “the site,” “DocsGPT” for the subject.
- **Active voice.** “Run the theme locally” not “The theme can be run locally.”
- **Scannable.** Tables, short paragraphs, clear headings. Same clarity principle as design system (readability first).
- **No marketing in technical docs.** Describe what something does and how to use it; don’t sell.

### UI and microcopy (buttons, labels, nav)

- **Short and action-oriented.** “Get in touch,” “Welcome,” “Get in Touch.”
- **Consistent terms.** Same label for the same action across pages (e.g. “Get in touch” as CTA).
- **No exclamation in UI.** Periods or no punctuation for buttons; avoid “Submit!” or “Let’s go!”

## 5. Do’s and don’ts

### Do

- Use **short sentences** for emphasis and rhythm.
- Lead with **what** and **why**, then detail.
- Use **concrete examples** (e.g. “API design,” “data pipelines,” “internal tooling”) instead of abstract “solutions.”
- Say **“we”** or **“I”** when speaking for the brand; **“you”** when addressing the reader.
- Keep **headlines and subheads** to one clear idea; support with body.
- Match **tone to channel**: slightly more personality on main site; neutral and clear in docs.

### Don’t

- Use **buzzwords**: “disruption,” “innovation,” “synergy,” “leverage,” “best-in-class,” “cutting-edge.”
- Use **startup jargon**: “move fast,” “scale,” “pivot,” “growth hack” (unless in a specific, grounded context).
- **Oversell** or use superlatives (“the best,” “unparalleled”).
- **Rant** or use excessive sarcasm, even in blog or social.
- Write **long blocks** without structure (headings, lists, short paragraphs).
- Use **corporate third person** (“Manny Roy Consulting provides…”); prefer first person or “we” where it fits.

## 6. Example phrases (from the site)

Use these as reference for tone and rhythm; adapt to context.

- **Positioning:** “Untangle complex technical and operational problems by building clear, sustainable systems rooted in data, collaboration, and measurable impact.”
- **Hero:** “Technical systems built to last.” / “Sustainable systems rooted in data, collaboration, and measurable impact.”
- **Tagline:** “Build once. Maintain well.”
- **About:** “Technical systems are rarely just technical. They’re operational, financial, human, and often quietly political.” / “The stack varies. The thinking doesn’t.”
- **Why we exist:** “Manny Roy Consulting exists to slow that moment down — to untangle before rebuilding, to design architecture before adding features.”
- **CTA:** “Let’s build something sustainable.” / “If you’re building something that needs to hold up over time, let’s talk.” / “If you’re navigating complexity or planning what’s next, I’m happy to talk.”
- **Contact:** “Let’s talk.” / “If you’re navigating complexity, planning a system redesign, or exploring applied AI, I’m happy to start the conversation.”

## 7. Emotional goal

After reading or using the site, the reader should feel:

- **Clarity** — They know what this is and whether it’s relevant.
- **Relief** — Complexity is named and addressed, not glossed over.
- **Confidence** — The brand feels capable and grounded.
- **Stability** — No hype; things are built to last.

**Not:** Overwhelmed, confused, or sold to.

Use this doc when drafting or editing any copy that represents Manny Roy Consulting. When in doubt, prefer clear and restrained over clever or salesy.
